## Risk Assessment
The main risks associated with these suppliers include:
* Supply chain risks: dependence on a single supplier, potential disruptions to the supply chain, and material shortages.
* Single-source risk: reliance on a single supplier for critical components, which can lead to supply chain disruptions and delays.
* Quality risks: potential defects or non-conformities in the products, which can impact the overall quality of the final product.
* Lead time risks: potential delays in delivery, which can impact production schedules and deadlines.

To mitigate these risks, it is recommended to:
* Diversify the supply chain by working with multiple suppliers.
* Develop a contingency plan to address potential supply chain disruptions.
* Implement quality control measures to ensure the quality of the products.
* Regularly review and update the supplier list to ensure that the suppliers meet the required standards.
