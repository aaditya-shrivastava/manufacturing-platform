## Risk Assessment
The main risks associated with the suppliers are:
* Supply chain risks: All suppliers are dependent on a global supply chain, which can be affected by factors such as natural disasters, trade wars, and pandemics.
* Single-source risk: Relying on a single supplier can be risky, as it can lead to supply chain disruptions and price increases.
* Quality risks: The quality of the products can vary depending on the supplier, and it is essential to ensure that the supplier has a strong quality control process in place.
* Pricing risks: The prices of the products can fluctuate depending on market conditions, and it is essential to negotiate a fair price with the supplier.

## Next Steps / Action Items
The next steps are:
1. Contact the recommended suppliers to discuss pricing and lead times in more detail.
2. Negotiate a contract with the selected supplier that includes pricing, lead times, and quality control measures.
3. Conduct regular audits to ensure that the supplier is meeting the agreed-upon quality and delivery standards.
4. Continuously monitor the market for new suppliers and technologies that can improve the quality and cost-effectiveness of the products.
5. Develop a risk management plan to mitigate the risks associated with the suppliers and the supply chain.
