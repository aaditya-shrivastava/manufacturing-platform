## Risk Assessment
The main risks associated with these suppliers are:
* Supply chain risks: dependence on a single supplier, potential disruptions to the supply chain, and fluctuations in raw material prices.
* Single-source risk: relying on a single supplier for a critical component or product.
* Quality risks: potential defects or non-conformities in the products, which could impact the overall quality of the final product.
* Lead time risks: potential delays in delivery, which could impact production schedules and timelines.

To mitigate these risks, it is recommended to:
* Diversify the supply chain by working with multiple suppliers.
* Develop a contingency plan to address potential disruptions to the supply chain.
* Implement a quality control process to ensure that products meet the required standards.
* Negotiate with suppliers to ensure that lead times are realistic and achievable.
